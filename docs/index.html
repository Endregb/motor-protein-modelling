Motor Protein Transport Simulation

Stochastic modeling of motor proteins using biased random walks in periodic potential landscapes

About this project: This simulation models the stochastic motion of motor proteins, such as myosin, as they move along intracellular structures. The project uses a biased random walk in a two-level periodic potential landscape to capture the essential features of directional movement, asymmetry, and stochasticity in biological systems.
